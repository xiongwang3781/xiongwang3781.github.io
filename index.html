Research

From mineral-scale fabrics to lithosphere-scale behavior

My work uses experimental and analytical approaches to reveal how minerals and rocks deform under high-temperature and high-pressure conditions.

01

Rock Rheology

Quantifying deformation mechanisms and rheological properties of crustal and mantle minerals under laboratory conditions.

02

Microstructure

Reading mineral fabrics, grain-scale textures, and deformation records to interpret mechanical history in natural and experimental samples.

03

EBSD / MTEX

Using high-resolution EBSD and MTEX workflows to connect crystallographic preferred orientation with seismic and rheological properties.

04

AI-Enhanced Coding

Building AI-assisted tools for faster, clearer, and more reproducible analysis of microstructure and deformation datasets.

AIMER / Code

AI-Driven Microstructure Evolution in Rheology

AIMER is an open-source direction for combining Earth-science domain knowledge, microstructure analysis, and AI-assisted programming. The current code materials include MATLAB workflows for deformation mechanism maps and experimental data visualization.

  • Deformation mechanism map generation for dry olivine.
  • Grain-size and differential-stress data overlays.
  • Reusable research-code patterns for transparent analysis.
